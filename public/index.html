§ II  /  Definition

What the show is,  and what it is not.

What the show is not NEG

  • Not a news-reactive politics show.
  • Not a generic current-affairs podcast.
  • Not a soft conversation about democracy detached from power, incentives, and institutional reality.
  • Not a vanity podcast built around guest prestige alone.
  • Not a vehicle for any party line, factional lens, or ideological tribe.

What the show is POS

  • A doctrine-driven platform for understanding what keeps civilizations free, capable, and able to defend themselves — and what causes them to weaken, decay, or become captured.
  • A serious conversation with dissidents, founders, investors, scientists, historians, policymakers, builders, and other high-agency people whose work explains how the world is changing.
  • An editorial project designed to leave listeners with a clearer map of reality, sharper judgment about what matters, and a deeper understanding of what serious renewal requires.
§ III  /  Doctrine

The Seven Pillars

01
Strength over Appeasement
Deterrence, hard power, and the will to defend a free civilization.
Power · Will
02
Truth over Propaganda
Epistemic integrity, media accountability, narrative warfare, and resistance to information capture.
Truth · Media
03
Merit over Mediocrity
Competence, standards, excellence, and institutional seriousness as survival requirements.
Standards
04
Capacity over Drift
State capacity, execution, infrastructure, and the ability to build, maintain, and govern.
State · Build
05
Borders with Legitimacy
Managed migration, civic coherence, democratic consent, and the conditions of durable belonging.
Consent
06
Freedom with Seriousness
Freedom that remains self-respecting, self-defending, and resistant to its own internal hollowing out.
Freedom
07
Renewal through Builders
Founders, scientists, institutions, educators, reformers, and individuals rebuilding what has decayed.
Renewal
§ IV  /  Host

About the host.

Mehdi Nayebi
Host & Creator

Entrepreneur, operator, and public thinker. The spine of the show.

Entrepreneur, public thinker, and host of Open Civilization. Born in Tehran, raised in France, and shaped by a decade in London finance at Deutsche Bank and Bank of America, he later built companies across Tehran, Dubai, and Toronto — and has spent much of his life thinking about what makes societies flourish, decay, or lose the will to defend themselves.

He co-founded Alopeyk, one of Iran's largest on-demand logistics platforms, and built inside a sanctioned authoritarian system before leaving Iran for safety reasons. That operational experience gives him an unusually credible perspective on authoritarianism, institutional dysfunction, state failure, and what it means to build under pressure.

His work sits at the intersection of geopolitics, institutions, technology, power, and civilizational renewal.

Come on the show.

Open Civilization hosts dissidents, founders, investors, scientists, historians, generals, technologists, policymakers, exiles, journalists, educators, and builders — anyone whose work helps the audience understand something important about power, institutions, freedom, capability, truth, decline, or renewal.
